This site was created to help students, lectures and many more learn how to use and implementsocial media tools into their learning. We aim to do this by explaining the basics and then also show you how to setup your accounts and showing you some basic ways to use your platforms for learning and teaching.
Social Media Tools Content Link
Snapchat Snapchat is an app that is available on the app store (apple) and the google play store (android). It is a simple app. Once you download it and make an account it is amazing what you can do! You can send and receive videos and photos from your ‘friends’ once you have added them. You can also post to your ‘story’ which is something that all your friends can see. Link
Instgram Instagram is a free social media platform which allows users to share pictures and videos with other users. Instagram allows it users to edit pictures and videos in app. Instagram allows users to ‘tag’ users in pictures and videos . Link
Tik Tok TikTok has changed the game for social media. It is a platform much like Instagram but JUST videos. Many people believe TikTok is brand new, but in actual fact it’s been around for much longer. Link
Twitter Twitter is an application that you can download on any android or apple phone through the app/play store for free. Link
YouTube YouTube began in 2005 and was started by Chad Hurley, Steve Chen and Jawed Karmin. It began to really take off from about 2006 onwards and since then has become one of the most common apps on every mobile or tablet device . Link
